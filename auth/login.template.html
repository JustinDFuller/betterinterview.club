Better Interviews

The Problem

Let's be honest: we're all biased and judgemental in our own way, even when we try hard not to be. It's an unfortunate quirk of humankind. It's particularly unfortunate when we try to evaluate others objectively because all sorts of unwanted bias sneaks in to our judgement.

This is problematic for those of us who conduct interviews. We want to hire the best candidates as quickly as possible. But, unnoticed by our conscious selves, we judge candidates based on their name, gender, race, age, accent, clothing style, zoom background, and more.

This is a pretty fun product pitch, isn't it?

The Solution

Luckily, we've known the solution for a long time. Countless people and organizations have discovered it over and over again.

To objectively assess a situation, we must ask consistent, unambiguous questions.

We must apply these same questions repeatedly, and they must be phrased with little room for subjective judgement.

Better Interviews does exactly that. It provides a framework that provides hiring managers and interviewers the tools they need to objectively asses candidates.

How It Works

It's pretty simple.

First, a hiring manager opens a role. They create five "yes" or "no" questions that interviewers will use to evaluate every candidate for the position.

Next, an interviewer receives a feedback request. After the interview, they select "yes" or "no" for each question.

There is an additional question, asking if the interviewer recommends the candidate.

The hiring manager will receive a summary of each submitted feedback.

Believe it or not, that's it. It's so simple, but I believe it will drastically improve your hiring experience.
